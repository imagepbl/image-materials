What is IMAGE Materials?
=================

IMAGE Materials is a dynamic material systems model developed in Python to simulate product stocks and flows, as well as the materials embedded within them, across time, regions, and sectors.
The model quantifies the evolution of material in-use stocks, inflows, and end-of-life outflows under different environmental assessment scenarios, such as those generated by the `IMAGE (Integrated Model to Assess the Global Environment) model`_.  

.. _IMAGE (Integrated Model to Assess the Global Environment) model: https://models.pbl.nl/image/Welcome_to_IMAGE_3.3_Documentation

It covers endogenously represented sectors within IMAGE (e.g., buildings, vehicles, electricity infrastructure), and other sectors, for which material requirements are estimated according to historical use patterns to close the global material demand.
End-of-life (EoL) flows are explicitly calculated, enabling the assessment of total waste generation, material recovery, reuse within sector, and recycling potentials. This structure allows IMAGE Materials to represent the full material life cycle, 
from production and use to disposal and recovery. This structure allows IMAGE and IMAGE Materials to represent material cycles from production and use to disposal and recovery. 

By linking material system dynamics with socio-economic and energy system pathways, IMAGE Materials supports the exploration of:

* Material efficiency strategies (e.g., product lifetime extension, product lightweighting, reuse, recycling);

* Sectoral interactions and material–energy feedbacks; and

* The implications of resource efficiency policies for greenhouse gas (GHG) mitigation pathways.

This integrated and comprehensive approach provides a consistent framework for analysing how changes in material use contribute to climate mitigation and resource demand reduction.
